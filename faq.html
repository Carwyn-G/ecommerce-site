Frequently Asked Questions

How long will my custom order take to make?

Production times vary depending on the order. In general, expect about 2-4 weeks for a throw size quilt, but many factors may add to this time. Some things that might increase your order's production time are if your quilt design requires specific fabrics that need special ordered, or has a particularly elaborate design. A more precise estimate will be provided when your custom order is confirmed. If you have any concerns about receiving your order in time, you are always welcome to reach out. While expedited production can't be guaranteed, it may be possible to speed up production slightly, but the earlier those needs are communicated the better.

How do I wash my quilt?

For any handmade quilt, less is more when it comes to washing. Overwashing can increase wear on the fabric and seams. For small stains, spot treat with gentle cleaning solutions and dabbing the stain. When a full wash is necessary, you can either hand wash or use a gentle wash cycle with cold water. To dry, gently squeeze out as much excess water as possible (do not twist or wring) and then lay flat to air dry. If possible, laying it outside on a sunny day can speed up drying, but anywhere you can lay the quilt flat will work.

Because of their extra bulk, puff quilts can take longer to dry and pockets of moisture can linger in the stuffing of the puffs. To prevent mildew forming inside the quilt, take care not to fold or store your puff quilt until you're sure it's fully dried.

How heavy are puff quilts?

A typical throw size puff quilt weighs about 10lbs (4.5kg). They are noticeably weightier than a traditional quilt or comforter, but not as heavy as a weighted blanket, making them a good option for anyone who finds a weighted blanket too cumbersome to maneuver but appreciates a bit of comforting weight from their blankets.

Are puff quilts very warm?

The warmth of a quilt can vary, but in general despite their added bulk a puff quilt is no warmer than a typical comforter or duvet. If you worry about overheating under your quilt, make sure to choose a quilt with a cotton backing. If you would prefer a quilt that is extra warm, choose a fleece or flannel backing.
